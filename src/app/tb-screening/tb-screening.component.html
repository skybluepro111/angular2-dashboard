TUBERCULOSIS SCREENING TOOL
AND RISK QUESTIONNAIRE

1.Has the patient been coughing for two weeks or more?      

2.Has the patient recently coughed blood in the sputum?

3.Has the patient experienced loss of appetite?

4.Has the patient lost weight of more than 3kg in the past four weeks?

5.Has the patient been sweating unusually at night?

6.Has the patient had recurrent fever /chills lasting more than three days?

7.Has the patient experienced chest pains, fast breathing and/or difficulty in breathing?

8.Does the patient have swellings in the neck, armpits or elsewhere?

If “Yes” to one or more of the questions, suspect TB, refer for further investigations OR investigate for TB e.g. sputum collection

COMPLEMENTARY QUESTIONS

9.i) Has the patient been treated for Tuberculosis?

  ii) When was the patient treated for Tuberculosis?

10.Was TB treatment completed?

11.Has the patient been in contact with someone diagnosed with Tuberculosis in the past year, e.g. same house hold or at work?

If “No” to all questions, patient is not a TB suspect. Repeat the screening in 3-6 months time.
